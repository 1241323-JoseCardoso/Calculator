# Glossary

# AISafe Glossary

| **TEA (EN)**                              | **TEA (PT)**                                         | **Description (EN)**                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
|:------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------|:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Aggregate                                 | Agregado                                             | A cluster of related domain objects treated as a single unit for data changes in DDD. Each aggregate enforces its own invariants and consistency rules. Each aggregate in the system must be documented with a representative sequence diagram.                                                              |
| Air Control Area                          | Área de Controlo Aéreo                               | A defined geographic region of airspace managed by a flight control entity. Each airport must be associated with exactly one air control area. Has a unique area code and valid geographic boundaries.                                                                                                       |
| Air Control Simulation                    | Simulação de Controlo Aéreo                          | A simulation that combines the execution of all flights within a given airspace area, including flights entering from other areas. Used to detect potential air safety violations (e.g., two flights in close proximity).                                                                                    |
| Air Traffic Control (ATC)                 | Controlo de Tráfego Aéreo (CTA)                      | The service responsible for managing aircraft movements in the airspace to prevent mid-air collisions and ensure safe, orderly flow of traffic.                                                                                                                                                              |
| Air Transport Company                     | Empresa de Transporte Aéreo                          | A company that uses the AISafe system to register aircraft and flights. Identified by a unique company name, IATA (2-letter) code, and ICAO code (2–3 letters).                                                                                                                                              |
| Air Transport Company Collaborator (ATCC) | Colaborador de Empresa de Transporte Aéreo (CETA)    | A collaborator of an air transport company registered in the system. Can register aircraft, flight routes, and manage pilots. Identified by email (valid company email), name, and position.                                                                                                                 |
| Aircraft                                  | Aeronave                                             | An aircraft of a specific model, operated by an air transport company. Characterised by a unique registration number, company, cabin configuration (number of seats per class), number of flight crew elements, and operational status.                                                                      |
| Aircraft Model                            | Modelo de Aeronave                                   | A motorized commercial aircraft design. Characterised by: Model ID, maker, type (passenger, cargo, mixed), motorisation, empty weight, MTOW, MZFW, maximum fuel capacity, service ceiling, cruise speed, wing area, drag coefficient (Cd), and lift coefficient (Cl).                                        |
| AISafe                                    | AISafe                                               | A startup developing a prototype flight control management system, targeting the air traffic control market.                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Airport                                   | Aeroporto                                            | A location characterised by: name, town, country, IATA airport code, ICAO airport code, coordinates, and altitude (elevation in metres above sea level). Must be associated with exactly one air control area.                                                                                               |
| Altitude Slot                             | Slot de Altitude                                     | A defined altitude band within a flight segment, characterised by a minimum altitude, maximum altitude, and width. An aircraft must fly within an allowed altitude slot when traversing a segment.                                                                                                           |
| AST (Abstract Syntax Tree)                | AST (Árvore de Sintaxe Abstrata)                     | An internal representation of a flight plan produced after lexical and syntactic analysis, capturing the structure and semantics of the DSL input.                                                                                                                                                           |
| Assessment Result                         | Resultado de Avaliação                               | The outcome of a skills assessment for an AISafe user. Possible values: PASSED, FAILED, or CONDITIONAL.                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Authentication & Authorization            | Autenticação e Autorização                           | Security mechanism enforcing that all system users must authenticate before performing any action, with role-based access control applied to all functionalities.                                                                                                                                            |
| Backoffice Operator                       | Operador de Backoffice                               | A system role responsible for feeding base configuration data into the system, including aircraft models, airports, air control areas, air transport companies, and engine models.                                                                                                                           |
| Bootstrap Process                         | Processo de Inicialização                            | An automated initialisation mechanism that pre-loads essential data into the system (e.g., users, airports, air control areas, aircraft models, engine models) without manual input, used to set up the system for operation.                                                                                |
| Cabin Configuration                       | Configuração de Cabine                               | The distribution of seats per class (Economy, Business, First) in a specific aircraft. The total number of seats cannot exceed the aircraft model's maximum passenger capacity.                                                                                                                              |
| Charter Flight                            | Voo Fretado                                          | A type of flight with a specific actual departure date, as opposed to a regular flight.                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Condition Variable                        | Variável de Condição                                 | A thread synchronisation mechanism used to signal state changes between threads in the parent simulation process. Used specifically to notify the report generation thread when a safety violation is detected.                                                                                              |
| Cruise Speed                              | Velocidade de Cruzeiro                               | The typical operating speed of an aircraft model, used in flight physics calculations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Decommissioned Aircraft                   | Aeronave Descomissionada                             | An aircraft that has been retired from service by an Air Transport Company Collaborator. A decommissioned aircraft cannot be assigned to new flights, and decommissioning is blocked if the aircraft has pending flights in the system.                                                                      |
| Departure / Arrival                       | Partida / Chegada                                    | Operational information associated with a flight leg, specifying the airport and time of departure or arrival.                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Drag Coefficient (Cd)                     | Coeficiente de Arrasto (Cd)                          | A dimensionless parameter characterising the aerodynamic drag of an aircraft model's wing profile. Used in the drag force formula.                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Empty Weight                              | Peso em Vazio                                        | The weight of an aircraft model without fuel, payload, or passengers.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Engine Maker                              | Fabricante de Motor                                  | A manufacturer of aircraft engines. Only basic information (name and country) is stored in the system.                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Engine Model                              | Modelo de Motor                                      | A specific engine design used by aircraft. Characterised by a unique combination of name and manufacturer, along with type, power, fuel, and efficiency data.                                                                                                                                                |
| FAA                                       | FAA                                                  | Federal Aviation Administration — an example of a flight control entity (public).                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Fleet                                     | Frota                                                | The collection of aircraft registered to a specific air transport company. Can be listed and filtered by aircraft model, maker, passenger capacity, or aircraft age.                                                                                                                                         |
| Flight                                    | Voo                                                  | A connection between two airports. Only direct flights are considered. Identified by a unique flight designator in the format `xxn(n)(n)(n)(a)` (airline designator + numeric flight number + optional operational suffix). Has a type (regular or charter), departure day/time, and scheduled arrival time. |
| Flight Block                              | Bloco de Voo                                         | The top-level structural unit of the Core Flight DSL. Wraps a complete flight operation and contains a unique flight identifier, a flight type, and one or more leg blocks.                                                                                                                                  |
| Flight Control Operator (FCO)             | Operador de Controlo de Voo (OCV)                    | A system user belonging to a flight control entity, directly managed by the system administrator. Responsible for testing flight plans and simulating air control.                                                                                                                                           |
| Flight Designator                         | Designador de Voo                                    | A unique identifier for a flight, composed of the airline designator (xx), a numeric flight number (n(n)(n)(n)), and an optional one-letter operational suffix (a). Full format: `xxn(n)(n)(n)(a)`.                                                                                                          |
| Flight Leg                                | Etapa de Voo                                         | A single, non-stop journey segment between two airports within a flight plan, defined by one take-off and one landing. Contains departure, arrival, route, one or more segments, and fuel information.                                                                                                       |
| Flight Plan                               | Plano de Voo                                         | A formal description of how a flight takes place, including the aircraft, departure date/time, fuel load, pilot, and route. Must be described using the Core Flight DSL. Status is set to "draft" until validated.                                                                                           |
| Flight Plan Status                        | Estado do Plano de Voo                               | The current lifecycle state of a flight plan. Set to "draft" upon creation. Updated after successful validation. Inserting new weather data into a previously tested plan voids the prior test result.                                                                                                       |
| Flight Process                            | Processo de Voo                                      | An independent OS process forked from the simulation parent process, responsible for executing one aircraft's flight plan during air control simulation. Communicates with the parent via a pipe and sends position updates at each time step.                                                               |
| Flight Route                              | Rota de Voo                                          | A named connection between two airports belonging to an air transport company. Named with 2-letter company initials and up to 4 digits (e.g., TP123). The name must be unique.                                                                                                                               |
| Flight Route Deactivation                 | Desativação de Rota de Voo                           | The process by which an Air Transport Company Collaborator deactivates a flight route from a given date onwards. No new flights may be created on the deactivated route. Deactivation is blocked if there are planned flights scheduled after that date.                                                     |
| Flight Simulation                         | Simulação de Voo                                     | A simulation of a flight plan to verify that the planned path and altitude are valid, and that the fuel quantity meets safety regulations. May use simplified or actual weather forecast data.                                                                                                               |
| Fuel                                      | Combustível                                          | The quantity of fuel loaded on an aircraft for a given flight leg. Must be strictly positive and associated with a specific leg.                                                                                                                                                                             |
| IATA Code                                 | Código IATA                                          | A 2-letter (company) or 3-letter (airport) code assigned by the International Air Transport Association. Must be unique.                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ICAO Code                                 | Código ICAO                                          | A 2–3-letter (company) or 4-letter (airport) code assigned by the International Civil Aviation Organisation. Must be unique.                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Inactive Pilot                            | Piloto Inativo                                       | A pilot who has been removed from active roster by an Air Transport Company Collaborator. An inactive pilot cannot be assigned to new flight plans. Deactivation is blocked if the pilot has flight plans currently assigned.                                                                                |
| Leg Block                                 | Bloco de Etapa                                       | A structural unit within a flight block in the Core Flight DSL representing a single non-stop flight phase. Must include departure, arrival, route, one or more segments, and fuel information.                                                                                                              |
| Lift Coefficient (Cl)                     | Coeficiente de Sustentação (Cl)                      | A dimensionless parameter characterising the aerodynamic lift of an aircraft model's wing profile. Used in the lift force formula.                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Maker                                     | Fabricante                                           | A manufacturer registered in the system. Can be an aircraft manufacturer, an engine manufacturer, or both. Only basic information is stored: name and country.                                                                                                                                               |
| Maximum Fuel Capacity                     | Capacidade Máxima de Combustível                     | The maximum volume or mass of fuel an aircraft model can carry.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Maximum Take-Off Weight (MTOW)            | Peso Máximo à Descolagem (PMD)                       | The maximum allowable weight of an aircraft at the moment of take-off, as defined by the aircraft model.                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Maximum Zero Fuel Weight (MZFW)           | Peso Máximo Sem Combustível (PMSC)                   | The maximum allowable weight of an aircraft excluding usable fuel, as defined by the aircraft model.                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| Motorisation                              | Motorização                                          | The type of engine used by an aircraft model. Types include: turboprop, turbofan, turbojet, ramjet, electric propeller, etc.                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Node / Junction                           | Nó / Junção                                          | A connection point between two or more flight segments, allowing the aircraft to transition between segments.                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Operational Status                        | Estado Operacional                                   | The current status of an aircraft in the system. Possible values: ACTIVE or DECOMMISSIONED. A decommissioned aircraft cannot be assigned to new flights.                                                                                                                                                     |
| Pending Flight                            | Voo Pendente                                         | A flight that has been registered in the system but has not yet been completed. An aircraft with pending flights cannot be decommissioned, and a pilot assigned to pending flight plans cannot be deactivated.                                                                                               |
| Pilot                                     | Piloto                                               | A system user certified to pilot one or more aircraft models. Belongs to an air transport company.                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Regular Flight                            | Voo Regular                                          | A type of flight that operates on specific days of the week, as opposed to a charter flight.                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Route                                     | Rota                                                 | A planned path for a flight between an origin and a destination airport. The same airport cannot be visited twice within a route. The origin must match the leg's departure airport and the destination must match the leg's arrival airport.                                                                |
| Security Clearance                        | Credencial de Segurança                              | A mandatory active clearance for AISafe users, which expires automatically at a given date. Managed by the Admin or Backoffice Operator. Possible clearance levels: LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, or CRITICAL.                                                                                                          |
| Segment                                   | Segmento                                             | A linear path connecting two geographic points (nodes) within a flight route. Characterised by: start coordinates, end coordinates, allowed altitude slots, width per altitude slot, and wind direction and speed (m/s).                                                                                     |
| Semantic Validation                       | Validação Semântica                                  | Post-syntactic validation ensuring domain coherence of a flight plan DSL file. Covers flight-level, leg-level, route, segment, and domain consistency rules. All errors must be collected and reported in a single execution.                                                                                |
| Semaphore                                 | Semáforo                                             | A synchronisation primitive used in the simulation to coordinate shared memory access across flight processes and parent threads, enforcing correct step-by-step simulation progression.                                                                                                                     |
| Service Ceiling                           | Teto de Serviço                                      | The maximum altitude at which an aircraft model can maintain a defined rate of climb.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Simulation Report                         | Relatório de Simulação                               | A file produced at the end of an air control simulation summarising: total number of flights, individual execution statuses, safety violation events with timestamps and positions, and an overall pass/fail validation result.                                                                              |
| Skills Assessment                         | Avaliação de Competências                            | A periodic evaluation (every 5 years per regulation) required for AISafe users, managed by the Admin or Backoffice Operator. Possible outcomes: PASSED, FAILED, or CONDITIONAL.                                                                                                                              |
| Termination Signal                        | Sinal de Terminação                                  | A signal sent by the parent simulation process to all flight processes when the number of safety violations exceeds the predefined threshold, triggering graceful cleanup and early termination of the simulation.                                                                                           |
| Thrust Specific Fuel Consumption (TSFC)   | Consumo Específico de Combustível por Impulso (TSFC) | A measure of engine fuel efficiency: fuel consumption (grams/second) per unit of thrust (kN). Used to estimate aircraft range during cruise flight.                                                                                                                                                          |
| User                                      | Utilizador                                           | A person with access to the AISafe system. Identified by a unique valid email from an allowed domain list, plus a name and phone number. Must authenticate to perform any action.                                                                                                                            |
| Valid Email Domain                        | Domínio de Email Válido                              | A system-defined list of permitted email domains. All user accounts must have an email address belonging to one of these allowed domains. Collaborator emails need not come from the customer's primary company domain.                                                                                      |
| Weather Data                              | Dados Meteorológicos                                 | Meteorological information registered in the system for a specific air control area and date. Used during flight simulation and flight plan validation.                                                                                                                                                      |
| Weather Person                            | Responsável Meteorológico                            | A system role responsible for registering and importing weather data into the system.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Weather Service                           | Serviço Meteorológico                                | An AI-enhanced service providing weather forecast data used by all system instances during flight simulation.                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Wing Area                                 | Área da Asa                                          | The reference surface area of an aircraft model's wings (m²), used in the lift and drag force formulas.                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
